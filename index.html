S A FFRON

Saffron is the world’s most precious spice.
For thousands of years, its red threads have connected
cultures through flavor, color, and trade.

Origin

Saffron comes from the stigma of a small purple crocus flower, known as Crocus sativus. It is harvested from the crocus flower, which blooms for only a few weeks each year.

The crocus originated in ancient Persia (modern-day Iran) and later spread across Asia and Europe through trade and cultivation.

DID YOU KNOW?

Each flower produces just three tiny red threads,
which are carefully handpicked and dried.
It takes around 150,000 flowers to produce
just one kilogram of saffron, making it one of
the most valuable substances on earth.

Journey
Across Continents

For over 3,000 years, saffron has traveled along
historic trade routes, linking civilizations from
Persia and India to Greece, Spain, and beyond.
It was prized not only as a spice but also as
a medicine, dye, and perfume.

In Persia, saffron symbolized joy and was
used in perfumes and celebrations.

In Spain, it gave the golden color and aroma
to paella, one of the country’s most famous foods.

In India, it became a sacred color of purity and
appears in religious rituals and traditional dishes.

Benefits

Beyond its beauty and flavor, saffron
offers many natural benefits.
It has been used in traditional medicine for
centuries and continues to be studied for
its health-promoting properties.

Taking saffron by mouth might improve some
symptoms of Alzheimer disease by a small
amount and improve symptoms of depression.

DID YOU KNOW?

When saffron is steeped in water, it
releases a deep golden hue.
This color has long symbolized warmth,
light, and life.

The pigment, called crocetin, was once used to
dye fabrics and manuscripts.
Its strong aroma and taste — slightly earthy,
floral, and sweet — make it a key ingredient in
both cuisine and culture.

From a single flower comes a thread that has colored the world for centuries.
Saffron continues to link past and present — through food, culture, and tradition.
It is truly the red thread of the world.
